"Is proportional to": This phrase indicates a relationship between variables. It means that whatever came before is equal to whatever comes afterward, but multiplied by a constant. This constant is called the constant of proportionality. In this context, this constant is called the gravitational constant and is denoted by the symbol \(G\text{.}\) Although Newton described this constant in 1687, it wasnâ€™t directly measured until Henry Cavendish did it in 1798.
