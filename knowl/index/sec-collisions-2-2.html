We will be using the principle of conservation of momentum to analyze what happens in collisions. This topic is usually presented with a discussion of the energy of the system, which breaks the world of collisions into two categories. If no energy is lost, then the collision is said to be elastic. These types of collisions basically do not exist in reality, and are considered to be a theoretical ideal. A collision in which energy is lost is said to be inelastic.
